# References — External Knowledge Base

Your brain doesn't just know about itself — it absorbs ideas from books, articles, conversations, and experiences. This module is where all that external input lives.

## What goes here

Everything you've read, watched, heard, or encountered that you want to remember and reference later:

- **books/** — Book notes, key takeaways, favorite quotes
- **articles/** — Articles, blog posts, tweets, forum posts
- **quotes/** — Single sentences or ideas that stuck with you
- **videos/** — Videos, podcasts, talks — with your notes
- **frameworks/** — Mental models, methodologies, thinking tools you've learned
- **inbox.md** — Quick dump. Throw anything here, sort later.

## What does NOT go here

- Your own decisions → `07-decisions/`
- Your own insights/lessons → `01-me/learned.md`
- Your own tasks → `05-tasks/`

**Rule of thumb**: If it came from outside you, it goes here. If it came from inside you, it goes in `01-me/` or `07-decisions/`.

## How to use

**Quick capture**: Just throw it into `inbox.md` with a source link. AI can help sort it later.

```markdown
- "The best time to plant a tree was 20 years ago" — Chinese proverb (saw on Twitter)
- Book rec from John: "Thinking in Systems" by Donella Meadows
- https://some-article.com — great framework for pricing open-source
```

**Organized entry**: When you have time, create a proper file:

```markdown
# books/thinking-in-systems.md
---
title: Thinking in Systems
author: Donella Meadows
date_read: 2026-03-15
tags: [systems-thinking, mental-models]
rating: 9/10
---

## Key Takeaways
- ...

## Favorite Quotes
- "..."

## How this applies to my work
- ...
```

## Layer

This is **Layer 2** — loaded on demand when you're discussing ideas, making decisions, or creating content. The AI can pull from your reference library to support arguments, find inspiration, or remind you of something you read.
