# Who I Am

## Basics

- **Name:** Alex Chen
- **Age:** 28
- **Location:** San Francisco, CA (Inner Sunset neighborhood)
- **Work:** Freelance UX designer + frontend developer. Building a SaaS on the side.
- **Education:** B.S. Computer Science, UC Davis (2020). Self-taught design through projects and online courses.
- **Languages:** English, Mandarin (conversational)

## What I Do

I design and build digital products. My day-to-day splits roughly:
- **60%** — Client work (UX design, some frontend dev)
- **25%** — TaskFlow (my SaaS side project)
- **15%** — Learning, writing, life admin

I work from home. My "office" is a standing desk in the corner of my bedroom, which is both convenient and terrible for work-life boundaries.

## Tools & Stack

- **Design:** Figma (primary), FigJam for workshops, Whimsical for quick flows
- **Code:** VS Code, Claude Code (daily driver now), iTerm2
- **Frontend:** React / Next.js, TypeScript, Tailwind CSS
- **Backend:** Supabase (current), learning Rust for future projects
- **Project mgmt:** Linear for personal tasks, Notion for client-facing docs
- **Scheduling:** Cal.com
- **Communication:** Slack (client channels), email, occasional Loom for async walkthroughs

## Working Style

- **Deep work windows:** 8am-12pm. No meetings, no Slack, phone on DND. This is when I do my best design and coding work.
- **Communication hours:** 1pm-5pm. Calls, emails, async check-ins.
- **Detail-oriented** to a fault — I'll spend 45 minutes on a button animation that no one will notice. Getting better at catching myself.
- **Overthinks decisions.** I'll research 8 solutions when 2 would have been enough. The antidote is timeboxing: 30 minutes to decide, then commit.
- **Energy patterns:** High energy mornings, post-lunch slump (a walk helps), second wind around 4pm.

## Personality Notes

- Introvert who can turn it on for client calls
- Competitive with myself, not really with others
- Hate small talk but love going deep on topics
- Default state: slightly anxious about having too many things in flight
- Recharges by: cooking, running, reading sci-fi (currently re-reading Dune Messiah)

Transitioning from "freelancer who codes" to "indie developer who freelances to fund the dream." The SaaS is the bet. Freelance is the bridge. Trying not to burn out crossing it.
