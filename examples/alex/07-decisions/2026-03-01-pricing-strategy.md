# Decision: Raise Freelance Rate to $150/hr

## Background

Been at $120/hr since I started freelancing in mid-2024. SF cost of living is brutal, and I've been undercharging relative to the value I deliver. Two trigger events:
1. Sarah (TechCorp) casually mentioned their last freelance designer charged $175/hr and "wasn't as good"
2. I calculated my effective hourly rate on the Finley project — it was $45/hr after all the unpaid revisions

## Options

1. **Stay at $120/hr** — Safe, no risk of losing clients
2. **Raise to $150/hr** — 25% increase, matches mid-market SF rate for UX designers
3. **Raise to $175/hr** — Aggressive, matches what Sarah mentioned. Might lose some pipeline.

## Decision

Going with **$150/hr**, effective March 1, 2026.

### Reasoning
- $150 is still below what top-tier SF freelance designers charge ($175-225)
- At $120 I was attracting budget-conscious clients who tend to be more demanding
- My portfolio is stronger now than when I set the $120 rate
- Two existing clients (Sarah, Marcus) both agreed without pushback
- $150 x 80 billable hrs/month = $12K/month, which covers SF rent + expenses with enough margin to fund TaskFlow development

### Implementation
- New clients: quote $150 from first conversation
- Existing clients: informed both Sarah and Marcus via email, gave 2 weeks notice. Both said "totally fair."
- Proposals: update all templates with new rate
- Next review: Q3 2026. If pipeline is strong and I have 3+ clients, consider $175.
