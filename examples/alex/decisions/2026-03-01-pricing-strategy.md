# Decision: Raise Freelance Rate to $165/hr

## Background

Been at $140/hr since I started freelancing after leaving Stripe. Portland cost of living is lower than SF but still not cheap, and I've been undercharging relative to the value I deliver. Two trigger events:
1. Sarah (DataForge) casually mentioned their last contractor charged $185/hr and "wasn't as reliable"
2. I calculated my effective hourly rate on the Finley project — it was $52/hr after all the unpaid scope creep

## Options

1. **Stay at $140/hr** — Safe, no risk of losing clients
2. **Raise to $165/hr** — 18% increase, matches mid-market Portland/remote rate for senior backend devs
3. **Raise to $185/hr** — Aggressive, matches what Sarah mentioned. Might lose some pipeline.

## Decision

Going with **$165/hr**, effective March 1, 2026.

### Reasoning
- $165 is still below what top-tier remote senior engineers charge ($185-250)
- At $140 I was attracting clients who wanted cheap and fast
- My portfolio is stronger now with the B2B SaaS niche
- Both existing clients (Sarah, Marcus) agreed without pushback
- $165 x 60 billable hrs/month = $9,900/month, which covers expenses with enough margin to fund CloudMetrics development

### Implementation
- New clients: quote $165 from first conversation
- Existing clients: informed both Sarah and Marcus via email, gave 2 weeks notice. Both said "totally fair."
- Proposals: update all templates with new rate
- Next review: Q3 2026. If pipeline is strong and I have 3+ clients, consider $185.
