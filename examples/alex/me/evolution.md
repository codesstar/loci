# Evolution Timeline

> Historical snapshots of how my thinking has changed. Only read when reviewing personal growth.

### 2026-03-01 | Pricing Confidence
Realized I was undercharging not because the market wouldn't pay more, but because I didn't believe I was worth more. When Sarah casually mentioned her previous contractor charged $185 and "wasn't as reliable," something clicked. Raised to $165 and nobody blinked. The bottleneck was my own self-perception, not the market. Next raise will be easier.

### 2026-02-15 | Builder Identity
Started thinking of myself as a "developer who builds products" instead of "a developer who writes code for clients." The distinction matters: a developer-for-hire writes features. A builder ships products that solve problems. CloudMetrics is the proof point — I'm not writing code to spec, I'm making decisions about what to build and why. Updated identity.md to reflect this shift.

### 2026-01-15 | Specialization > Generalism
Decided to stop taking "any project that pays" and focus only on backend/infrastructure work for B2B SaaS companies. This means saying no to ~40% of inbound requests, but the remaining work is more fulfilling, better-paying, and strategically valuable for CloudMetrics (I learn about real infrastructure pain points from every client). Short-term revenue hit was worth the long-term positioning.

### 2026-01-02 | Starting Loci
Set up this system as a way to externalize my thinking and give AI context about my life. The meta-insight: the act of writing things down forces clarity. Half the value of Loci is the writing, not the reading. Three months later, this is still true — the daily journaling habit alone has been worth the setup time.
